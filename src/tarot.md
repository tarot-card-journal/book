# Tarot Cards

## Introduction

Tarot cards are a popular divination tool used for fortune-telling and
self-reflection. They consist of 78 cards divided into two sets: the Major
Arcana and the Minor Arcana. The Major Arcana contains 22 cards representing
major life events and spiritual concepts, while the Minor Arcana consists of
56 cards divided into four suits (Wands, Cups, Swords, and Pentacles) with 14
cards each.

## Major Arcana

The Major Arcana are the 21 trump cards and the Fool card.
These are sometimes referred to as the greater secrets.

- The Fool
- The Magician
- The High Priestess
- The Empress
- The Emperor
- The Hierophant
- The Lovers
- The Chariot
- Strength
- The Hermit
- Wheel of Fortune
- Justice
- The Hanged Man
- Death
- Temperance
- The Devil
- The Tower
- The Star
- The Moon
- The Sun
- Judgement
- The World

## Minor Arcana

The Minor Arcana are the 56 cards divided into four suits: Wands, Cups, Swords,
and Pentacles.
Each suit has 14 cards, representing different aspects of life.
There are 10 numbered cards (1-10) and four court cards (Page, Knight, Queen,
King).

- Wands: 14 cards representing creativity, energy, and ambition.
- Cups: 14 cards representing emotions, relationships, and intuition.
- Swords: 14 cards representing communication, intellect, and conflict.
- Pentacles: 14 cards representing material wealth, stability, and practicality.

## Usage

To use tarot cards for divination, follow these steps:

1. **Shuffle the Deck**: Randomly shuffle the tarot deck to ensure randomness.
2. **Draw Cards**: Draw the required number of cards based on your query or for
   a spread.
3. **Interpretation**: Interpret the meaning of each card in relation to your
   question or the spread.
